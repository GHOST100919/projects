Lamborghini is one of the world's most iconic luxury sports car brands, but it began in an unexpected place—on a farm.
Founded in 1963 by Ferruccio Lamborghini, a successful tractor manufacturer, the company was born out of his frustration with
Ferrari’s customer service. Determined to build a better grand tourer, he launched Automobili Lamborghini in Sant’Agata Bolognese,
Italy.The brand gained early fame with the Miura in 1966, a revolutionary mid-engine supercar that set new standards in design and
performance. However, the 1973 oil crisis and financial struggles forced Ferruccio to sell the company, which changed hands several times
over the years.Lamborghini found new life in 1998 when it was acquired by Audi (Volkswagen Group). Backed by German engineering, the company
introduced modern classics like the Murciélago, Gallardo, Aventador, and Huracán. In 2018, it launched the Urus, a high-performance SUV, expanding
its reach.In 2023, Lamborghini entered the hybrid era with the Revuelto, combining electrification with its signature V12 power.From tractors to world-class
supercars, Lamborghini's journey is one of passion, innovation, and relentless pursuit of performance.
